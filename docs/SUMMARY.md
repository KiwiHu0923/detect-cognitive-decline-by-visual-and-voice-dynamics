# ParkScreen — Project Summary

**Built with Claude: Life Sciences Hackathon** (Anthropic × Gladstone × Cerebral Valley), July 7–13 2026 · Development Track

---

ParkScreen is a multimodal Parkinson's disease screening decision aid that fuses three task-matched recordings — sustained vowels (/i/, /o/, /u/), rapid syllable repetition (/pa-ta-ka/, the canonical clinical DDK task), and an 8–12 s smile-and-neutral video — into a single fused probability and a Claude-generated clinical-style report. The three channels target the three motor-speech and facial signs clinicians already look for: phonation quality (jitter, shimmer, HNR, F0), diadochokinetic rate and regularity, and facial hypomimia. On the [NeuroVoz](docs/DATASETS.md) analysis cohort (49 PD × 46 age-matched HC, N=95, subject-level LOSO), the fused speech classifier reaches **AUC 0.758 [95% CI 0.662, 0.859]**, versus DDK-only 0.740 and phonation-only 0.567 — the ablation confirms that channel fusion adds real signal, not just noise. The separately-trained facial channel reaches in-distribution AUROC 0.812 on UFNet's held-out participant split. Full 8-row ablation table + sensitivity analyses + cohort audit in [`docs/METHODOLOGY.md`](METHODOLOGY.md); raw numbers in [`eval/results/ablation_table.csv`](../eval/results/ablation_table.csv).

Architecturally, ParkScreen is two layers with different lifetimes. **Layer 1 (offline)** does score-level late fusion of two small, interpretable logistic-regression classifiers (one per speech channel) on NeuroVoz, with subject-level LOSO for evaluation — deliberately linear because N=95 makes deep learning overparameterized. **Layer 2 (real-time)** takes three task-matched user upload directories, scores each channel with the already-fitted Layer-1 classifiers plus the UFNet-trained facial classifier, fuses at score level with AUC-excess weighting, and passes the structured per-channel scores plus a hypomimia summary JSON into a frozen ~1143-token system prompt to Claude Opus 4.7, which produces a Markdown report with mandatory disclaimers, hedged language, and — critically — **channel-disagreement flags that are never silently reconciled**. Any missing channel returns N/A and the fusion renormalizes over the channels present. The end-to-end pipeline runs from Gradio UI or CLI; a full walkthrough is in [`demo/videos/parkscreen_demo.mp4`](../demo/videos/parkscreen_demo.mp4).

We are careful about what ParkScreen is *not*. It is a **screening decision aid, not a clinical diagnosis** — every report says so verbatim. Model calibration is valid only for ON-state PD versus age-matched HC (NeuroVoz PD subjects were recorded 2–5 h post-dose); the training cohort skews mild-to-moderate PD (Hoehn-Yahr stage 2 dominant); PD subjects average 5 years older than HC (p=0.013), so some fraction of the AUC is measuring age; and with N=95 the bootstrap 95% CI on AUC is roughly ±0.10. Training language is Spanish — vowels and PATAKA are language-neutral by construction, but cross-language transfer is untested. Every headline number in this repo is reproducible from commit `d935913` or later via the run order documented in the README. Full caveats: [`docs/LIMITATIONS.md`](LIMITATIONS.md).
